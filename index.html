Mosted visited place in the World

Switzerland is famous for its mountains, clocks and its food – especially cheese and chocolates. It’s a fantastic place for winter sports. The British Royals love to snowboard in Klosters while skiing in Zermatt gives you fantastic views of the iconic Matterhorn mountain.

Locations

Zurich

Zurich is full of surprises, from the Giacometti-designed police station to the ambitious Freitag Tower, a skyscraper made completely from recycled materials. This compact city is easily explored on foot, and if you’re looking for the quintessential Swiss experience, Altstadt (Old Town) doesn’t disappoint. Explore its medieval pathways and visit landmarks such as the FIFA World Football Museum and the upscale Bahnhofstrasse shopping street.

Lucerne

Lucerne is about as typically Swiss a city as you can get. Situated on the banks of the Alpine lake it takes its name from, the city has tons of charming medieval architecture, castles, famous clocks, chocolate factories and outdoor activities. There’s even an annual cheese festival, just in case all of that wasn’t Swiss enough for you. A genuinely beautiful city, with bundles of character among the narrow, cobbled streets of the Old Town, Lucerne is a delight to visit at any time of the year.

Geneva

French-speaking Geneva is a multicultural city located on the southwestern bank of Lake Geneva. Head down to the quays to marvel at the city’s surroundings and admire the soaring plume of the iconic Jet d’Eau fountain, one of the city’s most famous landmarks. Stroll along the cobblestoned streets in Geneva’s Old Town until you reach the 12th-century St. Pierre Cathedral, where Protestant Reformation leader John Calvin’s wooden chair is on display.

Grossmunster

Grossmünster is one of the city’s most important churches as it was the centre of Switzerland’s Protestant Reformation back in the 16th century. The Romanesque cathedral dates back to 1230, and was supposedly built on the graves of Zurich’s patron saints, Felix and Regula.The Reformation Museum occupies the church cloister, while the crypt hosts seasonal art exhibitions and literature events.

Swiss National Museum

The Swiss National Museum examines Zurich culture through a wealth of artefacts, from distant prehistory to the modern day. Founded in 1898, the museum occupies a turreted, neo-Gothic building between Platzspitz Park and Zurich Main Station. A tour through this museum’s extensive collection will take you to the medieval treasury's gem-encrusted relics, the armoury tower's jousting paraphernalia, and various archaeological discoveries – one of the museum’s most valuable relics is a 100,000-year-old flint stone.

Rieterpark

The city’s largest urban park, Rieterpark covers 70,000 sq m of green space near Lake Zürich. It’s a popular spot for recreational activities, including picnics, jogging, and sports. The area surrounding the park’s fountain is often used for classical concerts during summertime. Perched on a green hill above Lake Zürich, Museum Rietberg occupies an impressive neoclassical mansion, where Wagner once set Mathilde Wesendonck's poems to music. Today, the museum displays non-European works, including Tibetan bronzes, Indian miniatures, and Aztec sculptures.
